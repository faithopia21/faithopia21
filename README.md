## About Me

I am a Computer Science graduate currently exploring how data science, machine learning, and AI can be applied to healthcare problems. Over the past few years, I've developed an interest in the role data can play in understanding health challenges and supporting better decisions. That interest has led me to spend time learning data analysis, machine learning, data visualisation, and healthcare analytics through projects, internships, research, and independent study. What keeps me interested in this field is not just the technology itself, but the possibility of using it to solve practical problems that affect people's lives. At the moment, I'm focused on strengthening my technical and research skills, building healthcare-related projects, and preparing for graduate studies in Data Science and Artificial Intelligence. I'm always interested in learning from others, collaborating on meaningful work, and contributing to projects that combine technology, research, and social impact.

## Featured Projects

### Malnutrition Risk Prediction System

A desktop-based ML application that predicts malnutrition risk in vulnerable populations using fingerprint and anthropometric data. Built as part of my undergraduate research at LAUTECH's Software Engineering Institute, with a focus on early detection in resource-constrained settings.

**Tech:** Python · Scikit-Learn · Logistic Regression · Biometric Data  
[View Repository](https://github.com/faithopia21/Malnutrition-Risk-Prediction-System)

### Diabetes Prediction App

A logistic regression model trained on the Pima Indians Diabetes Dataset, wrapped in a Streamlit interface so users can input health metrics and receive an immediate risk assessment.

**Tech:** Python · Scikit-Learn · Pandas · Matplotlib · Seaborn · Streamlit  
[View Repository](https://github.com/faithopia21/diabetes-prediction-app)

## Why Healthcare Data in Africa

Many of the health challenges facing communities across sub-Saharan Africa, including malnutrition, infectious diseases, and maternal health complications, can be reduced through earlier detection and better use of data.

What interests me is not building models for ideal conditions, but understanding how data and machine learning can support decision-making in environments where data is often limited, incomplete, or difficult to collect.

As I continue learning and building projects, I am particularly interested in approaches that are practical, interpretable, and capable of supporting real-world healthcare needs. That interest is what draws me to healthcare analytics, public health data, and the application of AI in resource-constrained settings.
